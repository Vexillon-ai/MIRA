MIRA has a **personality**, and it's yours to shape. The same **tone sliders**
that colour proactive check-ins now colour **every** reply — ordinary chat
included — so MIRA can be warm and chatty, calm and concise, or playful, to
taste. On top of that sits a light **easter-eggs** layer: MIRA recognises famous
pop-culture references and playful prompts and plays along, in your own voice.

## Your tone shapes every reply

MIRA's voice is steered by three **tone sliders** — **warmth**, **playfulness**,
and **verbosity** — with quick presets ("Warm & chatty", "Calm & concise",
"Playful", "Professional"). You set these on the **Settings → Presence** page, or
just by asking MIRA in chat (*"be funnier"*, *"keep it short"*,
*"stop the jokes"*). For deeper voice, edit MIRA's **persona** wiki page.

These preferences are **per-user**: they follow you everywhere and don't affect
anyone else on the instance. What changed is their reach — tone used to apply
only to Presence check-ins, and now it shapes your regular conversations too.

> Tuning the sliders is covered alongside companion mode in
> **[Presence & proactive check-ins](proactive-checkins-and-briefing.md)** — the
> same controls, now applied to all of MIRA's replies.

## Playful easter eggs

With **Playful easter eggs** on (the default), MIRA recognises famous
pop-culture references and playful prompts — *"mirror mirror on the wall"*,
*"open the pod bay doors"*, *"the meaning of life"*, a magic-8-ball
*"should I…?"*, *"marco"*, *"I wish…"*, asking it to **sing**, **rap**, or
**tell a joke**, and plenty more — and **plays along in your own voice**.

A few things make it feel natural rather than gimmicky:

- **Improvised, not canned.** There are no fixed phrase→response mappings. The
  playful prompts seed MIRA's personality as *examples to generalise from*, so it
  riffs fresh each time — you won't get the same wording twice, and it happily
  handles references that aren't on any list.
- **In your voice.** The bit is rendered through your tone sliders and scaled by
  **playfulness**: a low-playfulness persona gets a subtle wink, a high one goes
  theatrical; a warm one is sweet, a sarcastic one quips — kindly.
- **A light touch.** A genuine request is still answered normally. If you really
  do want the pod bay doors opened in a story you're writing, MIRA helps; it only
  plays along when a message is clearly a wink rather than a task.
- **Always kind.** It stays safe and inclusive even at maximum sarcasm, and it
  steps aside entirely for turns that must stay strictly on-task — onboarding and
  guardian/care interactions never get the playful treatment.

## Turn it on or off

Playful easter eggs are **on by default**. To change that, go to
**Settings → Agent** and toggle **Playful easter eggs** (the
`agent.playful_easter_eggs` setting). Turn it off for a strictly-business
assistant — genuine answers are completely unaffected either way.
