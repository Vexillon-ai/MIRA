What makes MIRA different

Most AI assistants live in one app, on someone else's cloud, with a memory that resets every conversation. MIRA inverts all three.

📩 Reaches you on real channels

MIRA texts you on Signal or Telegram unprompted — a morning briefing, a check-in, a follow-up on something you asked about yesterday. Not a feed to open. A contact to read.

🧠 Grows a model of you

A markdown wiki you can hand-edit, plus a structured memory layer the agent updates as it learns. Every chat carries the same context — across channels, across months.

🏠 Runs on your hardware

One Rust binary. Linux, macOS, Windows. Your conversations live on disk you own; your provider keys never leave the box. Stop using it tomorrow and you keep everything that happened.

🛡️ Safety floor for the people you care about

Configure MIRA to check in on a loved one daily. If they don't reply within a window you set, MIRA pings a safety contact. The same person doesn't have to be both the user and the worrier.

🎙️ Voice in the box

Speech-to-text and text-to-speech ship with the binary — Whisper and Piper, no ffmpeg, no extra services. Send voice notes on Signal and MIRA replies with one back.

🤝 Multiple humans, one shared brain

Households and small teams can share one MIRA install. Each person's wiki and memories stay theirs; the persona stays consistent. No 30-seat-minimum enterprise sales call.

Self-host today — for free

MIRA is MIT-licensed and ships every release as a signed tarball. curl | sh installs the binary, registers a systemd / launchd / Windows service, and opens the onboarding flow in your browser. Bring your own API keys (OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, DeepSeek, Groq, xAI, OpenRouter, LM Studio, or fully local via Ollama) and MIRA is yours.

The catch: hosting it well needs a host. Domain, TLS, backups, provider key management, supervised process, browser push, signal-cli daemon — all doable, all takes an afternoon you might not have.
